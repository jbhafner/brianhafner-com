"How long until we show up in AI answers?" is the question I get most. The honest answer: you can go from completely invisible to genuinely recommended in about 90 days — if you run the right plays in the right order. Here is the exact sprint I use, phase by phase, so you can follow it yourself.

A quick word on why 90 days and not 900. AI visibility isn't a black box that takes years to crack. Most local and B2B service brands are invisible for boring, fixable reasons: their facts aren't machine-readable, they've never answered their customers' real questions in writing, and their proof of expertise is trapped in sales decks. Fix those three things systematically and the models respond faster than most people expect. Let's go.

## Before Day 1: set your baseline

You can't improve what you haven't measured. Spend an hour establishing where you stand today so you can prove movement later.

- Write the **15 questions** your best customers would actually ask an AI to find a business like yours.
- Ask each one in ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google's AI Overviews. Record whether you're named, who is named instead, and how accurate the answer is.
- Note your starting point: for most brands, it's "never mentioned." That's fine. That's the whole reason for the sprint.

## Phase 1 · Days 1–30: Fix the foundation

The first month is about becoming *legible* to machines. Nothing fancy — just making sure an AI can read who you are, where you work, and what you do without guessing.

- **Nail the fact layer.** Get your exact name, address, phone, hours, service areas, and specific services stated clearly on your core pages.
- **Add Tier 1 schema.** Organization, LocalBusiness, and Service markup in JSON-LD, validated with Google's Rich Results Test. Make sure the markup matches the visible page — models penalize mismatches.
- **Align your top three off-site profiles** (Google Business Profile, primary industry directory, main review site) so the core facts agree everywhere.
- **Add the cheap technical basics** — an `llms.txt` file and a clean sitemap. Low effort; take the free points, but don't expect miracles from them alone.

*End-of-phase check:* every core fact about your business is stated on-page and confirmed in schema, and it matches across your top profiles.

## Phase 2 · Days 31–60: Build the citable content

Now you give the models something worth quoting. This is where most of the visibility gains come from, because question-headed answers are the highest-citation format there is.

- **Publish 8–12 answer pages.** Take the real questions from your baseline and answer each one plainly, under a heading phrased the way a customer would ask it. Mark them up as FAQ where it fits.
- **Add proof of expertise (E-E-A-T).** Named authors, technician certifications and licenses, two or three real case studies with outcomes, client logos, and testimonials. Give the models a reason to trust you enough to say your name.
- **Go deep on one signature topic** — the thing you genuinely know better than anyone in your market — and become the most thorough resource on it.

*End-of-phase check:* a customer's top questions are answered on your site in language a model can lift, backed by visible proof you can deliver.

## Phase 3 · Days 61–90: Amplify, measure, and defend the win

The final month is about widening your footprint and connecting the work to revenue — so this becomes a channel you can defend, not a science project.

- **Earn third-party mentions.** A guest article, a local press mention, an association listing, a partner citation. Models triangulate — independent sources that corroborate you raise your credibility fast.
- **Re-run your baseline.** Ask the same 15 questions again. You're looking for movement from "never mentioned" to "sometimes cited," and from "generic answer" to "names us specifically."
- **Connect citations to pipeline.** Tie AI mentions to site visits, leads, and revenue in your reporting so you know — not hope — that it's working.

*End-of-phase check:* you're appearing in AI answers for real buyer questions, and you can trace those appearances to actual inbound activity.

## What realistic results look like

> 90 days doesn't make you the answer to every question. It makes you *an* answer to the questions that matter most — which, in a specific local or B2B category, is usually enough to win real deals.

Set expectations honestly. In a narrow category — "industrial boiler service in the Delaware Valley," "third-party management for mid-size multifamily in Tampa" — 90 focused days can move you into the recommended set for several of your highest-value queries. In a broad, crowded category it takes longer and the sprint becomes the first lap of a longer race. Either way, you finish the quarter measurably more visible than you started, with a system you can keep compounding.

## The one mistake that stalls every sprint

Doing the phases out of order. Teams get excited and rush to publish content before their fact layer is machine-readable — so the models can't connect the great answers to a real, locatable business. Foundation first, content second, amplification third. In that order, 90 days is genuinely enough to change your standing. Out of order, you'll spin for six months and wonder why nothing moved.
