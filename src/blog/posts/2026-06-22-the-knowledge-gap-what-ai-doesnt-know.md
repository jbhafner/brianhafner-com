If you're not showing up in AI answers, it's rarely because your business "isn't good."

It's usually because the AI systems **don't have enough reliable, specific information** about your brand—so they either skip you or cite competitors with clearer coverage.

This is the knowledge gap: the missing content that prevents AI from confidently mentioning you.

## What "AI Doesn't Know" Looks Like in the Real World

- Your brand is mentioned, but described inaccurately.
- Competitors get recommended for your use cases.
- AI gives generic advice without citing your resources.
- AI can't explain your process, pricing factors, or constraints.

## Why Knowledge Gaps Happen

- **Your site is brochure-style** (no direct answers).
- **Your niche isn't explicit** (category ambiguity).
- **Proof is missing** (no case studies, outcomes, reviews tied to services).
- **Key buyer questions aren't answered** (pricing, comparisons, "best for").
- **Third-party sources conflict** (directories/categories inconsistent).

## How to Identify Your Knowledge Gap (A Simple Method)

### Step 1: Run "Buyer Questions" prompts

Ask the questions your buyers ask:

- "Best [service] for [use case]"
- "[service] pricing in [city]"
- "[service] vs [alternative]"
- "How does [service] work?"

Record: Are you mentioned? Are you described correctly? Who is cited instead? What sources are used?

### Step 2: List the "missing answers"

When AI gives an incomplete answer, that's a content requirement. Common missing answers include:

- Clear definitions and scope
- Step-by-step process
- Requirements and constraints
- Pricing factors and ranges
- Comparisons and decision guides

### Step 3: Compare to competitors' content coverage

When competitors show up, they usually have at least one of these:

- A clean service page with FAQs
- Strong third-party citations/reviews
- Case studies
- Clear positioning around a niche

## Build a "Gap Map" (What to Create First)

Turn your findings into a prioritized map:

- **Tier 1 (money pages):** core services, highest intent queries
- **Tier 2 (decision support):** pricing factors, comparisons, FAQs
- **Tier 3 (authority builders):** guides, glossaries, proof libraries

## Quick Wins That Close the Biggest Gaps

- Add 8–12 FAQs to your top service page
- Publish one "pricing factors" page (even without exact prices)
- Create one comparison page (X vs Y)
- Publish one case study with measurable outcomes
- Fix category and service descriptions across major profiles

## Bottom Line

If AI doesn't mention you, it's often because the information needed to recommend you doesn't exist in a reliable, extractable format. Identify the gaps, prioritize them, and publish with intent.
